Text 1 – Food, Farming and Our Planet

Many people today are concerned about the food they eat. The way our food is produced is an important factor for the health of our planet. Factory farming, for example, produces large quantities of meat and dairy products very cheaply. However, it also releases enormous amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. Scientists have announced that agriculture is responsible for nearly a quarter of all global emissions.

A conventional diet that includes a lot of red meat is not only bad for the climate – it can also cause people to become overweight. Nevertheless, many customers find it difficult to change their habits because fast food is simply too convenient. The convenience of ordering a delivery from an app is hard to resist. Experts state that we should combine a healthier diet with smarter shopping choices.

Products like cocoa are often grown on plantations that destroy rainforests. We can do without many of these products or choose fair-trade alternatives. It is our duty to make better choices – because it truly matters.

Text 2 – Energy, Technology and the Future

One of the most efficient ways to fight climate change is to switch from fossil fuels to renewable energy. Wind farms and solar panels can generate electricity without releasing harmful greenhouse gases. Governments around the world have begun to announce ambitious targets: many old coal power stations are being shut down and replaced by cleaner technical solutions.

Smart devices connected by cables and wireless networks offer broad possibilities for managing energy at home. A new method called "demand response" allows electricity companies to encourage customers to use less power during peak hours. Experts predict that eventually every household will use such systems. Droughts caused by climate change also make it necessary to set clear targets for water-saving devices.

Mobility is another key issue. Electric cars are becoming more available, and many employers now offer their staff e-bikes or public transport tickets. The debate is often framed as comfort versus sustainability – but the two do not have to be opposites. Every move counts, and every vote matters. Remember: "Your time is up." – the planet cannot wait any longer.

Text 3 – Health, Cosmetics and Responsibility

The beauty industry uses a broad range of ingredients, some of which come from endangered ecosystems. Many cosmetics contain palm oil grown on plantations that were once tropical forests. Good health care should not come at the expense of nature. Scientists are developing new methods to create products that suit consumers without harming the environment. Some drugs are also tested on animals raised in poor conditions, which raises serious emotional and ethical questions.

From childhood onwards, people are influenced by advertising. We hardly think about where our products come from. But it matters – for our planet, for equal rights of farmers and workers, and for our own health. After a long day, we may feel exhausted, but we should still address these issues. Our mood affects our choices – and our choices affect the world. Some companies engage in fraud by labelling products as "green" when they are not. Always be to the point when asking questions, and make sure that equal standards apply to all.
